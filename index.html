Who We Are

Africa’s food systems are under strain.

Every year, millions of tons of food are lost after harvest. Farmers struggle to access reliable markets. Supply chains remain inefficient and fragmented. Food insecurity persists despite enormous agricultural potential. Meanwhile, universities and researchers continue to generate powerful ideas that never reach implementation.

This is not a problem of potential.

It is a problem of pathways. Africa does not lack intelligence, creativity, or innovation. What it lacks are systems that help ideas survive long enough to become real solutions.

Founder's Perspective

BioNovAA began from a personal conviction. As someone standing at the intersection of food science, technology, software engineering, and data analysis, I have seen both the depth of the problems and the scale of the opportunity.

Africa’s food systems will not transform by accident. They will transform because people choose to build the systems that enable change.

BioNovAA is one of those systems. It is not built all at once. It is being built step by step, through experimentation, learning, and continuous improvement. This is infrastructure in progress.

Continuous Innovation

BioNovAA is designed to prevent idea loss. We build infrastructure, not just events. After each challenge, we host post-innovation programs to support promising solutions.

Innovation Sprint Labs

Short, structured sessions where teams refine ideas, validate assumptions, and improve feasibility.

Continuation Support

Helping teams continue development instead of stopping at early ideation.

Debate Series

Structured discussions exploring critical food systems questions with experts and students.

Founder Check-Ins

Supporting innovators continuously through the tough early stages of development.
